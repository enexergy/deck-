Investment Thesis

Why this, why now.

The global energy order has fractured. Nord Stream is gone. Qatari LNG capacity has been destroyed. NATO's security umbrella is under question. US LNG exports have surged tenfold in a decade — not as a sign of market health, but as a sign of a market in crisis.

Every energy vulnerability exposed by these events shares one characteristic: dependency on something outside national borders. A pipeline crossing international territory. An LNG tanker route through contested waters. A supplier relationship built on a geopolitical alliance that no longer holds.

Enxergy has none of those vulnerabilities. By design. The feedstock is domestic plastic waste — generated within the UK, processed within the UK, supplying energy within the UK. The entire supply chain operates inside a single sovereign jurisdiction.

The most valuable energy asset in the world right now is one that nobody else can turn off. That is what Enxergy is building.

Geopolitical Case

The world changed.
Enxergy was built for it.

The assumptions that underpinned energy security for 30 years have collapsed in real time. Energy is no longer a commodity. It is a geopolitical weapon, a national security asset, and the single most contested resource on the planet.

US oil exports surged from under 500,000 barrels per day in 2016 to 5.2 million barrels per day in April 2026 — a tenfold increase in a decade. The US is now the world's largest LNG exporter, having filled the vacuum left by destroyed supply routes and collapsed supplier relationships. This is not market health. It is market crisis.

The most valuable energy asset in the world right now is one that nobody else can turn off. That is what Enxergy is.

Insurance and financing costs for energy infrastructure with foreign supply dependencies are rising. Infrastructure with genuinely domestic supply chains is becoming materially cheaper to insure and finance — a structural cost advantage that compounds over time. Enxergy is not chasing that trend. It was structured before it became obvious.

Nord Stream Sabotage
Germany went overnight from energy dependency on Russian gas through fixed infrastructure to scrambling for US LNG at significantly higher cost. A single event destroyed decades of energy infrastructure and forced an entire continent to rethink its supply strategy.
Qatari LNG Facility Destruction
Further concentrated global LNG supply dependency on the US, with US LNG exports to the Middle East increasing 147% as a direct consequence. The Middle East — historically a net energy exporter — is now partially dependent on US energy imports.
NATO Cohesion Under Pressure
US withdrawal from or significant reduction of its NATO commitment removes the security umbrella that underpinned European energy infrastructure assumptions for 75 years. Energy sovereignty is now a genuine national security imperative.

Pre-IPO Position

The gap between
what you paid and what it's worth.

Pre-IPO means the investor acquires a position before the company becomes publicly traded. The entry price is set by private negotiation at current project valuation — not by the open market. The open market has not yet had the opportunity to price this asset.

When it lists, the market will price the combined entity based on what Enxergy actually is at that point — an operational, revenue-generating plant that has completed its build, commenced production, and demonstrated that the model works in practice. The market will price the shares as an infrastructure asset in a sector that has never been more strategically important.

The pre-IPO investor paid for the project before any of that value was visible. The gap between what they paid and what the market subsequently values it at is the return. That gap is structural — it exists because public markets price demonstrated, measurable value. They cannot price operational value that does not yet exist.

The pre-IPO investor is compensated for two things simultaneously: taking on the construction and execution risk the public market will not take on, and providing capital at a stage when the project needs it most. That compensation is a lower entry price than the post-listing market price — permanently.

Exit Mechanism Already Defined
Merger with a company already listed on the London Stock Exchange. The listing pathway does not need to be created or approved — it already exists. This removes the single greatest source of exit uncertainty in pre-IPO investment.

Infrastructure, Not a Startup
When Enxergy lists, the market prices a physical energy plant with proven output capacity — not a technology bet dependent on future customer acquisition. Infrastructure assets with demonstrable output trade at significant multiples in public markets.
The Window Is Structural, Not Manufactured
The pre-IPO window closes when the listing occurs. Not because anyone decided to close it — because the event that defined the window has ceased to exist. The company is listed. Pre-IPO pricing, by definition, is no longer available. This is not a sales tactic. It is a mathematical reality.
Entry Price Rises at Every Stage
As each milestone passes — capital raise completes, construction begins, plant progresses — the risk profile improves and the justified valuation rises. The investor who comes in today pays the pre-construction price. Every stage that completes after entry increases the value of the position without further action required.
